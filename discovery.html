Artists on Art: John Heiss on Charles Ives

The Delayed Public Discovery of Charles Ives:


  • 1887, Charles Ives writes his first song "Slow March" at age 12.

  • 1892-1912, the majority of Ives' works are composed.

  • mid-1920s, Ives virtually ceases to write new music.

  • 1939, premiere of Piano Sonata No. 2, Concord, Mass. 1840-60 approximately 24 years after Ives completed it. Ives is awarded the Pulitzer Prize for this work the following year.

  • 1954, Ives dies at age 79.

  • 1962, John Heiss hears Ives' music for the first time. He would begin digging into the music in the mid-1970s.
